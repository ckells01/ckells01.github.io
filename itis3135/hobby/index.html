Music and Concerts

About Me

My name is Connor Kells, and I am a junior at the University of North Carolina at Charlotte. I am a huge music fan, and listening to music and going to see live music is a big hobby of mine. I played music in my middle school and high school bands, and I considered joinng the UNCC marching band, but the time commitment was too much for me. I regularly go to concerts whenevr I am able to and I listen to music frequently throughout the day. I took a music class my frshman year of college, and considered getting a music minor, but it did not end up working out because of the classes I wanted to take.

My Introduction to Music

I have loved music for a long time. My parents both played instruments growing up. My mom played panio and my dad played trombone and drums. He actually still plays drums frequently in the soundproofed practice room we have in our basement. Starting when I was a kid, my parents would have music on frequently and would encourage my brother and I to listen to what we enjoyed. My dad would also take my brother and I to concerts we were interested in going to see, which definitely helped me grow to love music and concerts.

I went to my first realy concert when I was 7 or 8, and have been going frequently ever since. I go to even more shows now that I am an adult and can go own my own. Getting into music at a young age helped me make the choice to join the band in middle school. This was when I learned that I enjoyed playing music as well as listening to it. I am now a huge music fan in general, even though I am no longer in a band.

Playing Music

I was hesitent to join the school band when I started middle school, but my parents convinced mw that it was worth at least trying it, saying that if I didnt enjoy it after the first year I could always choose a different class. I joined the middle school band and picked the clarinet as my starting instrument. I enjoyed the clainet alot, but it was pretty small and sometimes a few of the note were difficult for me to play because my hands are very big and it was causing issues for me. I switched to the bass clarinet, which is played the same way but it is alot larger. It is an octave lower than the normal clarinet, which I thought was very cool. I was selected for the All-County Honors band in middle school, and selected for the All-District Honors band in twice in high school. I also qualified to audition for the All-State Honors band in high school.

The highest level band at my high school was also the marching band. The band did not march with bass clarients, so I decided to play percussion during marching season. I did this for all three years in was in the marching band. Our band did very well, and won many of the competitions we went to. When the band did parades each year (the Raleigh Christmas parade and for school events) I would march with symbols, as the percusion pit was not involved in parades, and is seprate from the drumline.

Favorite Genres

My favorite genre of music by far is heavy metal. I have been listening to metal for years, as my dad's favorite type of music is also metal. Because of this, I grew up listening to heavy metal bands like Iron Maiden, Fates Warning, and Colossus. Iron Maiden was one of the first metal bands I saw live, and it was an amazing show. A lot of people who like metal do not like the heiver side of the genre, so nothing heavier than thrash metal bands like Megadeth and Testament (both are awesome live!).

After listening to metal for over a decade though, I do now enjoy alot of heavier sub-genres. This was not always the case, but in the last few years I have started to branch out more. These heavier subgenres include speed metal, thrash metal, melodic death metal, melodic black metal, and some older normal death metal bands. I am still not a huge fan of things like grindcore though, which includes bands like Exhumed and Cattle Decapitation. The heaviest genre I enjoy is deathcore, but only specific bands, such as Thy Art is Murder.

Favorite Bands

Most of my favorite bands are from the genres mentioned in the "Favorite Genres" tab. Some of my favorite NWOBHM (New Wave of British Heavy Metal) bands include Iron Maiden, Spellcaster and Judas Priest. I also listen to alot of thrash metal bands. Some of my favoirites include Slayer, Testament, Havok, and Kreator.

Some of my other favorite bands are actually from North Carolina. This means I am able to see them play live frequently, which I love. These include Mega Colossus, Knightmare, Children of the Reptile, Walpurgus, and Twisted Tower Dire. Most of these bands are NWOBHM bands. This means that they have a similar style to bands like Iron Maiden and Jusdia Priest, which I listed as some of my other favorites. I belive that I have seen Mega Colossus live more times than any other band by far. I have been going to their concerts for years.
